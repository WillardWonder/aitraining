Example 2: Documentation – Turn What You Know Into a Reusable Template

Why This Matters in Manufacturing Offices

Many office processes—like creating purchase requests, updating reports, or managing vendor issues—are done from memory, shared verbally, or hidden in old emails. That leads to inconsistent results, missed steps, and knowledge loss when someone’s out.

You Might Need Documentation If:

  • People frequently ask you how to do something
  • You wish there were written instructions for what you do
  • You manage a task that involves handoffs between roles
  • You’ve taken over a responsibility but no one left notes

Step 1: Describe a Process in Plain Language

What to Do: Think of a task or process you’re responsible for. Describe it like you would to a new coworker shadowing you. You're not writing a document yet—just explaining what happens.

  • What the process is for
  • Who’s involved
  • How it starts and ends
  • What tools or systems are used
Prompt to Enter in ChatGPT:
Describe your process in plain language.

Then paste your plain-language description.

After ChatGPT responds:

  • Read the output. Does it look like a document?
  • Would someone else understand it?
  • Ask ChatGPT for edits: "Break this into steps" or "Make it easier to follow."

Step 2: Use a Structured Prompt to Create a Better Document

Now try again with more direction. This gives ChatGPT context and formatting guidance to return a usable document.

Structured Prompt:
You are a documentation assistant. I’m creating a reusable document that describes a task or process in our office. Here’s the description of the process: [Paste your updated process description here.] Please return a clear, structured document with the following: - A title placeholder - Section headers with short instructions - Friendly but professional formatting - Areas people can fill in - Highlight anything people often forget - Suggest any related documentation that may be needed

After the output:

  • Check for clarity and usefulness
  • Ask for adjustments: tone, layout, wording
  • Use "Edit in Canvas" if available to refine in real time

Step 3: Save and Reuse Your Template

Ask ChatGPT to convert the final document into a reusable template with placeholders and instructional tips.

You can now:

  • Save it as a Word doc or PDF
  • Store it in a shared folder or SOP library
  • Use it as a printable reference or editable file

Teach ChatGPT to reuse it:

This is our preferred documentation format. Use this every time I ask for a guide or SOP unless I say otherwise.

Advanced Option – Build a CustomGPT:

  • Upload your structure and instructions, and prompt the guide to create a document assistant at chat.openai.com/create
  • Build a private assistant that always uses your tone, format, and sections. You can upload your template as a knowledge base file.
